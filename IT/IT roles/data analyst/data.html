Responsibilities


Responsibilities of Data Analyst

Data Collection and Analysis

Data collection and examination, gathering relevant information from multiple sources, structuring it for effective analysis, and deriving meaningful insights.

Database Management and Maintenance

Maintaining and managing databases, creating and managing databases to store and organize data effectively, and ensuring data integrity.

Generating Reports and Presenting Findings

Generating comprehensive reports highlighting key findings and trends, and presenting insights in a way that's understandable and valuable to stakeholders.

Data Visualization and Dashboard Creation

Creating visual narratives that convey complex insights in a simplified, easily digestible manner, using tools and software to build interactive dashboards and graphical representations.

Who can apply for this role?

IT Degrees:

Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA): Provides a strong foundation in programming languages, databases, and data structures.

Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Computer Science (CS): Offers a comprehensive understanding of algorithms, data structures, and programming.

Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Information Technology (IT) or Computer Science (CS): Delve deeper into technical aspects, providing a solid foundation for data analysis.

Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Data Science or Business Analytics: Equip graduates with advanced data analysis skills.

Non-IT Degrees:

Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com.): Focuses on statistics, mathematics, and business fundamentals.

Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) in Mathematics, Statistics, or Economics: Provide strong mathematical and analytical skills.

Note:

A relevant degree can be helpful, but practical skills like data wrangling, programming, and critical thinking are also essential.

Strong communication and problem-solving skills are also necessary for data analysts.
